Financial Management

Maintaining financial records is a vital part of monitoring business performance. Record keeping is essential for planning and managing of your business. This will also assist in meeting the taxation obligations for your business. Correct and up-to-date financial daily records provide the required information for managing the company efficiently and making comprehensive choices.

Recording your finances

To correctly maintain daily financial records it is recommended to:
Establish a record keeping system either manual or electronic.
Business transactions to be recorded accurately and promptly
At the end of each month, a summary account should be prepared, including income and expenditure.

Identify and rectify all discrepancies or errors in documentation or financial transactions immediately.
Discrepancies may relate to: errors in transposing numbers or journal entries; interest charges; bank errors or charges; or dishonoured cheques.

Policies & procedures that may affect records:

Designated timelines; guidelines for reconciling journals; legal and organisational policies, procedures and requirements; OHS/WHS guidelines; quality assurance; procedural manuals; security protocols; resolution procedures

Records to retain: 

Sales and expenses receipts and invoices; cash register tapes; bank statements including any loans or credit card statements; employee records like tax file declarations, time sheets, superannuation records and salary and wages ;

Logbooks for motor vehicles; depreciating assets information; stocktake records of inventory; business asset purchased like computer equipment, office furniture; debtors and creditors lists and records

Accurate financial account keeping is a cornerstone of a successful business.
Account keeping is the recording of various financial transactions between your business and your customers, and your business and your suppliers.

Cash and accruals accounting

Cash Accounting:
Cash accounting is the simplest accounting type and involves all cash received to be recorded at the time it is received and all expenses paid to be recorded at the time that the expense is paid.

Accruals Accounting:
Revenue or income that is received by the business will be recorded as they are earned rather than at the actual time that the cash was received.
