 Small Business Cash Flow & Cash Control

Cash Flow 

Cash flow is imperative when it comes to the financial managing of your business.
Cash flow management is the solution for the problem of the gap between the time of having to pay your suppliers and employees, and the time payment is collected from your customers. Cash flow management means postponing outlays of cash as long as possible while encouraging customers that owe you money to pay it as promptly as possible.
You may have assets, income and profits. However, if you run out of cash, everything stops. To prevent a cash predicament and guarantee that you always have a minimum amount of cash, you must know in advance how much cash will be leftover. You need to follow some common-sense rules to ensure cash flow is accurately monitored.
It is crucial understanding that income and expenses are not necessarily cash. If you sell goods on credit, your profit and loss statement shows this as income, but it is not cash until you collect. Also, buying goods on credit is an expense, but is not a cash reduction until you pay.
The process to monitor cash flow starts with forecasting. 

Budgeting 

The basics of budgeting - critical for small business sustainability 

Budgeting is the process of creating a plan to spend your money. This spending plan is called a budget. Creating this spending plan allows you to determine in advance whether you will have enough money to do the things you need to do or would like to do.
Using a realistic personal and business budget to forecast your spending for the year can really help you with your long term financial planning.
You can then make realistic assumptions about your annual income and expenses and plan for your long term financial goals.

The Goal of Investing

The goal of investing is to build wealth over time and to generate income from your investments to meet your financial goals and objectives. Investors purchase assets with the expectation that the value of these assets will increase and that their financial goals will be realised. Successful investing requires time, patience and a clear and realistic plan directed towards your goal.

There are three factors that can affect the likelihood that your financial goals and objectives are met:
your time horizon
the rate of return on your investments
and the amount you invest

Sound Financial Management 

The stronger your financial management the greater the opportunity you have to maximise your profits in the short term and to grow your capital value in the long term. The easier it is for you to raise finance, and probably at a lower cost. 

Good financial management leads to a transparency of figures and supports a real understanding of the numbers in your business.
It makes it much easier to find savings, to show others how profitable your business is and simply allows you to sleep much easier at night.
